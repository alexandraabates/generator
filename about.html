Generator Residency

About Us

Generator was founded on a single conviction: that sustained time and genuine freedom are the scarcest resources in an artist's life, and the most important ones to protect.

Mission

Why Generator exists

The art world runs on deadlines, grant cycles, residency applications, production schedules, and the constant pressure to produce work that fits legible categories. These structures have their uses, but they also create a kind of chronic urgency that leaves little room for the slow, uncertain work of genuine inquiry.


Generator was built to interrupt that rhythm. We offer eight weeks in which the only obligation is to be fully present to the questions that matter to you. No output is required. No exhibition is planned. The work you make here belongs entirely to you.


We believe that conditions matter — that the physical environment, the quality of attention, the company of peers, and the absence of commercial pressure all shape what it is possible to make. We have designed Generator to provide the best possible conditions we know how to create.

"The work that changed the most wasn't the work we planned — it was the work that surprised us. We want to build the conditions in which that surprise becomes possible."


— Eleanor Marsh, Founder

What We Believe

Our values

Time is the medium

We don't truncate residencies to reduce cost. Eight weeks is the minimum in which we believe real transformation is possible. The schedule is designed to front-load orientation and back-load reflection, protecting the middle weeks as a space of pure production.

No output requirements

We never ask residents to produce a specific body of work, show work in progress, or justify how they spent their time. The only obligation is to show up, be present, and participate in community dinners twice a week.

Radical accessibility

The stipend, housing, and materials fund exist to ensure that a Generator residency is available to artists regardless of economic circumstance. We do not charge a program fee. We do not require a specific educational background or career stage.

Community over institution

We are small by design. Twelve residents per cohort is the largest group in which we believe genuine intimacy is possible. The alumni network is the most valuable thing Generator produces — a community of practitioners who share a particular experience of creative freedom.

History

How we got here

  • 2019 Generator founded by Eleanor Marsh and Thomas Osei in a former textile mill in Hudson, NY. First cohort of six residents, spring and fall sessions.
  • 2020 Program paused in spring; fall cohort held with modified in-person protocols. New outdoor studio spaces added. Stipend increased to $4,000.
  • 2021 Full return to three annual cohorts. Cohort size expanded to twelve. Digital fabrication lab added. First alumni exhibition at a partnering institution.
  • 2022 New housing wing completed, allowing all twelve residents to live on-site. Mentorship program formalized. Stipend raised to $5,000.
  • 2023 50th cohort resident welcomed. Materials fund introduced. First Generator Prize awarded to an outstanding alumna, Miriam Kowalski.
  • 2024 Ceramics studio and darkroom completed. Stipend raised to $6,000. Partnership with three international institutions to offer exchange residencies.
  • 2025 100th resident welcomed. Endowment campaign launched to secure Generator's long-term independence. Applications for 2026 now open.

Thomas oversees the physical campus, staff, and logistics that make Generator run. He trained as an architect, and the design of the studios reflects his conviction that space is an argument about what matters.

Ana manages the application process, resident support, and community programming. She holds a degree in arts administration and previously worked at a literary residency in Vermont.

Generator Residency

The Program

Eight weeks of uninterrupted creative research, structured mentorship, and peer exchange — designed for artists at a critical inflection point.

What It Is

A residency built around the work

Generator is a full-time residency for practicing artists, writers, and interdisciplinary makers. Residents receive unstructured studio time, a living stipend, access to shared fabrication facilities, and a cohort of peers committed to rigorous creative exchange.


We believe the conditions of production matter. Generator strips away the pressures of commercial deadlines and replaces them with space — physical, temporal, and intellectual — to pursue the questions that matter most.

Structure

Three pillars

01

Unstructured Time

Five days a week, the studio is yours. No deliverables, no progress reports, no obligations beyond the work itself. We provide the space and get out of the way.

02

Mentorship

Each resident is paired with a senior practitioner for the duration of the residency. Weekly one-on-ones, informal studio visits, and a closing critique with the full cohort.

03

Peer Exchange

Cohort dinners on Tuesdays and Thursdays. An optional mid-residency open studio. A final group presentation open to alumni and the wider Generator community.

04

Facilities

24/7 access to private studios, a shared woodshop, a digital fabrication lab with laser cutters and CNC router, a ceramics studio, and a fully equipped darkroom.

05

Housing

Private on-site housing for residents who need it. Common kitchen and living spaces are shared. The campus is a 90-minute train ride from Penn Station.

06

Materials Fund

A $1,200 materials budget, disbursed at the start of the residency. No receipts required, no restrictions on use. It's yours to spend as the work demands.

Questions

Frequently asked

Who is eligible to apply?

Generator is open to artists, writers, and interdisciplinary practitioners at any career stage. There are no degree requirements. We do not accept students currently enrolled in degree programs. International applicants are welcome; we do not sponsor visas.

Is attendance required for the full eight weeks?

Yes. The residency is a full-time commitment. Residents are expected to be on campus for the duration of their cohort. We are unable to accommodate part-time or remote participation.

Can I bring my family or partner?

On-site housing is for residents only. Partners, children, and pets are not able to stay on campus, though visitors are welcome for day visits on weekends.

What does the application require?

A portfolio of recent work (10–20 images or equivalent), a project proposal of no more than 600 words describing what you intend to work on during the residency, a CV, and two letters of support. No application fee.

Does Generator retain rights to work produced?

No. All work produced during the residency belongs entirely to the resident. We ask only that residents acknowledge Generator in public presentations of work developed here.
